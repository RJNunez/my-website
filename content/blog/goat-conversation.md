## The Greatest Of All Time (The G.O.A.T)

The debate over basketball's greatest player has been going on forever. After LeBron
James missed the playoffs in 2018–19, a lot of people who are prisoners of the moment
started arguing about who the best current player is. My own view: none of them are at
LeBron's level yet. Those conversations revived the older, bigger debate — who is the
greatest of all time?

To keep this simple, I looked at three players: Michael Jordan, Kobe Bryant, and LeBron
James. The data come from [Basketball-Reference](https://www.basketball-reference.com).
LeBron's data run through February 21, 2019 (that's when I pulled them), so the tail end
of the 2018–19 season isn't included. Jordan's and Bryant's data are complete.

## Key achievements compared

**Michael Jordan** won six NBA championships and never lost a Finals series, taking
Finals MVP in all six. He won five regular-season MVPs.

**Kobe Bryant** won five championships but only two Finals MVPs — his first three titles'
Finals MVPs went to teammate Shaquille O'Neal. He won one regular-season MVP.

**LeBron James** won three championships, taking Finals MVP in each. He earned four
regular-season MVP awards, and many argue he deserved more.

## Statistical analysis

- **Points per game:** Jordan led in most of his career years, though James stayed
  remarkably consistent at roughly 27 PPG.
- **Assists per game:** James substantially outpaced both of the others.
- **Field goal percentage:** James was the most efficient, hovering around 55% after
  his 10th year.
- **Free throw percentage:** Jordan and Bryant were both clearly better than James here —
  the one legitimate statistical critique of LeBron.
- **Three-point percentage:** All three were broadly comparable.

## Cumulative records

James leads in career assists (8,512 and counting) and, based on a simple linear model,
looked set to become the NBA's all-time leading scorer within about three more seasons.

## Conclusion

If it were up to me, I'd give the G.O.A.T title to LeBron James — his consistency across
every category is unmatched. Jordan comes second and Bryant is a very close third. I'll
confess I'm a Kobe fan, but the data don't lie.
